Research Focus

I am an interdisciplinary researcher combining formal training in Computer Science with advanced literary studies in Urdu, Arabic, and Persian. My mission is to bridge traditional philology with data-driven methods to preserve the Urdu poetic canon.

Recognizing that classical Urdu is a computationally "low-resource" tradition, I have developed a comprehensive framework to digitize manuscripts, process complex morphologies, and enable large-scale literary analysis.

Research Methodology: 3-Tier Framework

My proposed approach follows a three-tiered pipeline to transform raw manuscripts into scholarly knowledge.

Tier 1: Data Curation 1

Building the foundational infrastructure for digital preservation.

  • UrduLit-Corpus: TEI-XML encoded datasets.
  • Sources: Rekhta.org, Archive.org, printed manuscripts.
  • Processing: Tesseract Urdu OCR + Manual verification.
  • Metadata: Full standards compliance for searchability.
  • Access: Open access and ethically sourced.

Tier 2: Tool Development 2

Developing specialized NLP tools for low-resource languages.

  • UrduNLP-Toolkit: Modular Python library.
  • Tokenizer: Handles RTL script & poetic compound words.
  • Tagging: Fine-tuned transformers for POS & NER.
  • Meter Analysis: Automated 'Arūż classification.
  • Infrastructure: Docker-ready & reproducible.

Tier 3: Literary Analysis 3

Applying computational methods to extract literary insights.

  • Stylometry: PCA, t-SNE, clustering for authorship.
  • Thematics: LDA & BERTopic for pattern discovery.
  • Sentiment: Emotion analysis with Urdu lexicons.
  • Validation: Collaboration with literary scholars.
  • Output: Scholarly publications & visualizations.
